Happiness

The visualisations below show how multiple factors such as the economy, health and trust of the government affects the happiness of the people.

Happiness Around The World

The figure on the left represents the happiness index in every country. Red represents that the happiness index is VERY LOW, Yellow represents that the happiness index is MODERATE and GREEN represents that the happiness index is High. According to the choropleth map, West side of the map seems to be generally happier than the East side of the map.

Top 5 Happiest Countries in 2015

The bar graph shows the top 5 happiest countries in the world in 2015. Switzerland was the happiest country in 2015 based on the happiness score. It is followed by Iceland in second, Denmakr in third, Norway in fourth and Canda in fifth. Hover your mouse on the graph to see each country's happiness score in 2015.

How Happiness Affects Health

The scatterplot aims to provide evidence that happiness and health co-relates with each other. We can see an increase in health (life expectency) of a country when there is an increase in the happiness index of the country. Thus, the state of our health DOES affect our happiness as humans.

Economy and Happiness

The economy of a country plays a big role in a country as it determines whether a country is prosperous or not. According to the line chart on the right, the economy (GDP) of a country does affect the people's happiness. This is proven by a linear regression line plotted. The calculated goodness-of-fit (R-squared) is 0.61 (seen on the top right of the chart) that shows that there is a strong relationship between Economy (GDP per Capita) and Happiness Levels.

Generosity and Happiness

Scientific studies from Nature.com and medicalnewstoday.com states that being generous may tend to lead you to a happier life. However, being generous might play a less crutial role compared to the economy in being happy. A scatterplot of generosity against happiness score is plotted along side with a linear regression line and a R-squared value. According to the visualisations observed, the R-squared value obtained from Generosity against Happiness Score is 0.03 while the R-squared value obtained from the graph of Economy against Happiness score is 0.61. This proves that the Economy plays a more important role in being happy as compared to being generous.

Donut Chart of Happiest Region throughout 2015 to 2019

The donut chart shows the happiest region from 2015 to 2019. Top 10 happiest countries of each year is extracted and a donut chart is produced. Western Europe has the majority of the chart with 72% followed by Australia & New Zealand with 18% and North America with 10%.

Slope chart of happiness score of 5 countries

A slope chart of Year against Happiness Score is plot that only shows 5 countries (Australia, Malaysia, Indonesia, Finland and United States). According to the chart, happiness levels of Indonesia, Malaysia and United States had drastically decreased from 2015 to 2019. Happiness level of Australia decreased slightly and happiness level of Finland has greatly increased.
